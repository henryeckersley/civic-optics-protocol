# Sample Audit – Georgia absentee‑voting pages

This sample demonstrates how the Civic Optics Protocol can be applied to real civic information.  Three Georgia absentee‑voting pages were audited in April 2026.  Each page was scored according to the five COP‑1 criteria.  Scores were multiplied by their respective weights to produce a Civic Optics Index (COI) on a 0–100 scale.

## 1. Georgia.gov – “Vote by Absentee Ballot”

* **Legibility (8/10)** – The state guide uses high‑contrast fonts, clear headings and bullet lists.  Long paragraphs are broken into numbered steps, making it easy to read.
* **Understandability (7/10)** – Instructions explain who can vote absentee and list the various submission methods, though some legal jargon remains.  The page notes that an absentee application must be submitted and that you must be registered【436236394389441†L68-L101】.
* **Navigability (8/10)** – The page provides a table of contents and links directly to the application portal and county registrar search【436236394389441†L94-L104】.  Navigation within the site is straightforward.
* **Currency (8/10)** – The content reflects the law allowing applications between **78 and 11 days before Election Day**【436236394389441†L101-L104】, but no explicit “last updated” date is displayed.
* **Actionability (9/10)** – Step‑by‑step instructions tell the reader exactly how to request, print, sign and submit an application【436236394389441†L115-L147】.

**COI:** 78.5/100

## 2. Columbus GA – “Absentee Voting”

* **Legibility (8/10)** – The Muscogee County page uses clear section headings and numbered substeps.
* **Understandability (8/10)** – It clearly outlines the five steps from verifying registration through mailing back the ballot【141463244600308†L31-L50】.
* **Navigability (9/10)** – All submission options (online, mail, fax, email, in person) are enumerated with corresponding links or instructions【141463244600308†L47-L73】.
* **Currency (9/10)** – The site notes that applications can be requested from **78 days before an election until the Friday before Election Day**【141463244600308†L47-L50】 and includes dates for the 2026 election cycle.
* **Actionability (8/10)** – Provides contact information, mailing address and instructions for tracking ballot status【141463244600308†L89-L114】.

**COI:** 83.5/100

## 3. Laurens County – “Absentee Ballot Packet Instructions & Returns”

* **Legibility (7/10)** – The page uses bullet lists but lacks strong typographic hierarchy.  Some navigation elements are hidden behind collapsed menus.
* **Understandability (7/10)** – It describes how to package the ballot and emphasises that ballots will not be rejected if the inner envelope is omitted【645421985989424†L31-L39】.
* **Navigability (6/10)** – The page structure is less intuitive; dynamic menus can make information harder to find.
* **Currency (7/10)** – The drop‑box location and hours are specified【645421985989424†L42-L49】 but there is no update date.
* **Actionability (8/10)** – It lists who is legally allowed to deliver a ballot【645421985989424†L52-L61】 and warns about legal penalties【645421985989424†L63-L71】.

**COI:** 69.5/100

## Summary

The audit shows that state and county election offices provide detailed absentee‑voting instructions, but there is variation in legibility and navigability.  Pages that break content into numbered steps and offer clear links to forms scored higher.  Providing an explicit “last updated” date would improve the currency scores.  Additional plain‑language summaries could raise understandability.
